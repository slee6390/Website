Neuroscience

Neuroscience is not just a subject; it's a world of wonder where I first found my academic passion. During my time at the University of Southern California, I dove headfirst into the captivating world of the brain. It all began with my undergraduate major and my early research, where I unraveled the intricate connections between genetics and behavior in mice with high-risk autism genes. But the real spark for this journey was ignited long before, through my heartfelt interactions with children with neurodevelopmental disorders. Their unique personalities and experiences were my inspiration. Today, my fascination with the human mind continues as I work with individuals on the schizophrenia spectrum. It's a journey of discovery and compassion that keeps me enthralled in the world of neuroscience.

Cognitive Science

Enter the enchanting realm of cognitive science. It's like stepping into the vibrant garden of the human mind, where thoughts and actions bloom with endless wonder. During my time at USC, I embraced this fascinating field as one of my majors. My passion for unraveling the intricacies of human cognition led me to explore not only the depths of the mind but also the captivating intersections of philosophy and linguistics. Cognitive science isn't just about answers; it's about asking the right questions and connecting the threads that weave our understanding of the human experience.

Computer Science

Ah, the world of coding and logic, it's like a bright and delightful puzzle! My journey into computer science started with an introductory coding class during my time at USC, and I couldn't help but fall for it. Those weekends spent on coding projects? They turned from moments of frustration into charms. Coding, I realize, is like a language of its own, a unique way to weave your thoughts into the digital canvas. It's amazing how a few lines of code can open up an entirely new world of possibilities.

Healthcare

My experiences working with children who have neurodevelopmental disorders have left an indelible mark. I remember how their smiles would brighten when I connected with their unique routines and habits, meeting them where they were. Those moments were pure sunshine, a testament to the power of understanding. These lessons apply equally to my interactions with adults on the schizophrenia spectrum and even neurotypical individuals. The ability to bridge communication gaps and truly connect with others remains my driving force. Academic research and healthcare both play vital roles in this journey, each contributing in their own special way. The impactful work in healthcare leaves me not only impressed but also deeply inspired, growing my interest to this ever-evolving field.
